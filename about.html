About The Intersectional Business Academy :

The Intersectional Business Academy was founded with one purpose: to make leadership, learning, and business growth accessible for those who juggle it all—busy professionals, entrepreneurs, parents, and community leaders.

2015 - The Spark :

What began as a local initiative to support women leaders grew into a full business academy dedicated to practical leadership education, inclusive systems design, and professional growth.

Our Story

Founded on the belief that true success blends strategy with humanity, The Intersectional Business Academy emerged as a space where busy leaders and aspiring entrepreneurs could find tools, mentorship, and community support.

Over the years, we have guided hundreds of professionals through transformative programs—supporting startups, advancing women in leadership, shaping inclusive workplace systems, and helping organizations align business intelligence with ethics.

2015 - The Beginning

We started as a mastermind group for women leaders who wanted more than traditional networking. We focused on practical education, courage in leadership, and building confidence in business.

2018 - Expanding Horizons

As word spread, our academy grew to support entrepreneurs, professionals, and teams seeking inclusive strategies for growth. This period marked our expansion into leadership training, organizational development, and ethical business consulting.

2023 - Today

Now, The Intersectional Business Academy offers programs for individuals and organizations across industries—helping them achieve measurable growth while staying true to their values.

Our Philosophy:

We believe leadership is intersectional—it must recognize the whole person and the multiple roles they play. We teach strategies that are both data-informed and emotionally intelligent, blending business rigor with empathy.

Our philosophy is simple: growth should not come at the expense of well-being or values. Every leader deserves access to tools that empower them to thrive at work, at home, and in their communities.
